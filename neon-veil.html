the brief

Setting the scene.

NEØN VEIL needed a visual identity that matched their sound — underground, atmospheric, and electric. The brand had to feel like stepping into a neon-lit venue at 2am: dark edges, glowing accents, and an intensity that lingers.

The challenge was to make it feel raw and underground without becoming illegible or trend-reliant.

the concept

Underground.
Electric.
Atmospheric.

The brand lives in the tension between darkness and neon light. The visual language borrows from underground rave culture, editorial fashion, and the kind of graphic design you'd find on a flyer stuffed under a windshield wiper at midnight.

Typography was treated as texture — stacked, distorted, and layered over imagery to create a sense of depth and urgency.

type direction

Letters as texture.

The primary typeface is sharp, condensed, and unapologetic. Paired with a secondary grotesque for body text and data — the combination reads as both industrial and refined.

Tracking was pushed wide on short labels and display text, pulled tight on headlines to create visual mass.
